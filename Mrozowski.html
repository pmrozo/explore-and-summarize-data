Bivariate Analysis

Here we begin bivariate analysis. The structure of dataset is simple. From one side we have Countries, from the other we have rows/columns with cell phone quantities since 1965 till 2011 as separated values. So actually to begin analysis we must create some new variables like average/total amount by binding some data.We are going to compare overall data set characteristics with my country Poland.

We want to answer a few questions: 1- how the total amount of phones changed over time 2- how the total amount of phones changed in Poland and compare it with global analysis 3- how is the relation between world trend to my countrys trend

Since the data is in separated columns we must build two variables: years and total

Now we can narrow this growth to one country, e.g.Poland and compare this with total value for all dataset.

##    Poland 
## 370378624

Now lets visualize cell quantity in Poland.

We can see that significant increase occured in 1995 till 2005.

Now lets change scale to see it better.

We can now compare both values and avarage in the world: plots on one graph to see how Poland is related to overall total number

We can see that the shape is different and the increase of phones tends to slow down from 2008 and 2009 . Now lets change Y scale in the same way we did before.

We can see that the shape of function is similar but is relatively x times less (obvious) but also started about 10 years after worlds trend, which is justified taking into account the political aspect of Poland. Year 1989 is actually the fists year of capitalism and end of communism, so country coud benefit from hith tech that was forbidden before.

Reflection

By anlysing the data we can see the very beginning of cellular telephony in 80 growing steadily with a boom in late 90 and in the next decade. Poland was 10 years behind worlds trend until 1989 . Today Poland is actually the leader of LTE technology in Europe by having the most users and signal coverage. First difficulty I had was related to the data itself. I had to create csv file with header and import it properly to R.
